% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection*{Problem setting}

A datacenter draws a flat $P = 1{,}000$\,MW at all $T = 8{,}760$ hours/yr.
Regulation requires that fraction $x \in (0,1)$ of demand be sourced
\emph{on-site} each hour; the rest may be imported at an exogenous hourly spot
price $p_t$ (DKK/MWh~$\div$~7.46 = €/MWh). This is the \textbf{CFE
constraint}: grid purchases $g^+_t \leq \bar{g} = (1-x)\cdot P$ every hour.
Three on-site options are compared on \textbf{total annualised cost}:

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep,topsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{KK} — 1\,GW SMR, constant output, one planned outage of $10\%$
        of the year placed optimally in the price series.
  \item \textbf{VE} — jointly optimised solar PV, onshore wind, and BESS.
        Single LP over capacities and all 8{,}760-h dispatch simultaneously.
  \item \textbf{VEGAS} — VE + gas turbine (CCGT, green gas). Gas is last in
        the merit order. Single joint MILP with McCormick linearisation enforcing
        a 40\,\% minimum-load constraint on the gas turbine.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{VE — single LP}

\textbf{Variables.} $3 + 8T \approx 70{,}083$ decision variables (sh\,=\,4 fixed;
setting sh\,=\,\texttt{None} adds $E_b$ as a fourth free capacity variable):
$c_s,\,c_w,\,P_b$ (capacities) plus eight blocks of $T$ dispatch variables.
All continuous and non-negative.

\textbf{Objective.}

\begin{equation}
\min \;
  \alpha_s c_s + \alpha_w c_w + \beta_P P_b + \beta_E E_b
  + \sum_{t=1}^{T}
  \!\Bigl[
    (p_t + \tau^+)\,g^+_t
    - (p_t - \tau^-)\,g^-_t
    - \eta\,q^w_t
    + M\,e_t
  \Bigr]
\label{eq:ve_obj}
\end{equation}

Annualised capacity cost coefficients ($\text{CRF}(r,n)=r(1+r)^n/[(1+r)^n-1]$, $r=4\%$):
$\alpha_s = \text{capex}_s\cdot\text{CRF}_s + \text{opex}^f_s$;\;
$\alpha_w = \text{capex}_w\cdot\text{CRF}_w + \text{opex}^f_w + \eta\sum_t\text{CF}^w_t$;\;
$\beta_P = \text{capex}^P_b\cdot\text{CRF}_b + \text{opex}^f_b$;\;
$\beta_E = \text{capex}^E_b\cdot\text{CRF}_b$.

$\alpha_w$ charges wind O\&M on gross generation; $-\eta\,q^w_t$ credits back curtailed
hours, so only dispatched MWh pay the 1.98\,€/MWh. Solar O\&M is zero, so the LP
always curtails wind before solar.

\textbf{Constraints.}

\begin{align}
  c_s\,\text{CF}^s_t + c_w\,\text{CF}^w_t
    + d_t - u_t + g^+_t - g^-_t - q^s_t - q^w_t &= P
  && \forall\,t \quad\text{(energy balance)} \label{eq:bal}\\[2pt]
  s_t - s_{t-1} &= \eta_c\,u_t - d_t/\eta_d
  && \forall\,t \quad\text{(SOC recurrence)} \label{eq:soc}\\[2pt]
  g^+_t - e_t &\leq \bar{g}
  && \forall\,t \quad\text{(CFE cap)} \label{eq:cfe}\\[2pt]
  u_t \leq P_b,\;\; d_t \leq P_b,\;\; s_t &\leq E_b
  && \forall\,t \quad\text{(battery limits)} \label{eq:batt}\\[2pt]
  q^s_t \leq c_s\,\text{CF}^s_t,\;\; q^w_t &\leq c_w\,\text{CF}^w_t
  && \forall\,t \quad\text{(curtailment limits)} \label{eq:curt}\\[2pt]
  g^-_t &\leq \bar{g}
  && \forall\,t \quad\text{(export cap)}
\end{align}

\textbf{Cyclic SOC.} The L matrix in \eqref{eq:soc} has $L_{0,T-1}=-1$, making the
implicit prior SOC at $t=0$ equal to $s_{T-1}$: the battery closes the year at the same
charge level it started, with no net energy accumulation.

\textbf{LP feasibility.} The box bound is $g^+_t \leq P$ (not $\bar{g}$); the CFE slack
$e_t$ absorbs violations at penalty $M=10^6$\,€/MWh, ensuring the LP is always feasible
during the simultaneous capacity-and-dispatch solve. At the optimum $e_t=0$.

\textbf{Capacity caps.} Optional parameters \texttt{max\_solar\_mw} and
\texttt{max\_wind\_mw} add bounds $c_s \leq \overline{c}_s$ and $c_w \leq
\overline{c}_w$ directly in the LP, enabling sensitivity analysis on land
availability and grid connection constraints (solar: 100\,km²/GW at
10\,ha/MW).

\textbf{Solver.} Assembled in \texttt{scipy.sparse} CSR format ($2T$ equality rows,
$6T$ inequality rows, ${\sim}70{,}084$ variables) and passed to \textbf{HiGHS} via
\texttt{scipy.optimize.linprog(..., method='highs')}. Solves in seconds on a single
thread.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection*{KK benchmark}

No LP is needed. Output is constant at 1{,}000\,MW; the only decision is where to
place the $\delta T = 876$-hour maintenance window.

\textbf{Outage placement.} The window is placed at the cheapest contiguous block:
$k^* = \arg\min_k \sum_{t=k}^{k+875}(p_t+\tau^+)$, found in $O(T)$ via cumulative sum.

\textbf{CFE exemption.} During the outage the datacenter imports 1{,}000\,MW —
above $\bar{g}$ — and the hourly CFE constraint is waived (planned maintenance
exemption). Grid connection is sized for 1{,}000\,MW import regardless of $x$.

\textbf{Total cost.}
\[
  C_{\text{KK}} =
    \text{capex}_{\text{KK}}\cdot\text{CRF}_{\text{KK}}\cdot P
    + \text{opex}^v_{\text{KK}}\cdot P(1-\delta)T
    + \sum_{t \in \mathcal{O}} (p_t + \tau^+)\cdot P
    + \text{gc}(P)
\]

\subsection*{VEGAS — VE + gas turbine, single joint MILP}

\textbf{Setup.} A CCGT on green gas is added as a fourth on-site source. At
$\approx$115\,€/MWh variable cost it is last in the merit order; it fires only during
Dunkelflaute when VE and battery cannot meet the CFE floor.

\textbf{Problem.} The VE LP is extended with a fifth capacity variable $c_g$, hourly
dispatch $z_t$, and binary on/off $o_t \in \{0,1\}$. The minimum-load constraint
$z_t \in \{0\} \cup [\mu c_g, c_g]$ ($\mu = 0.40$) involves the bilinear product
$c_g \cdot o_t$, which is non-convex when $c_g$ is also a decision variable.

\textbf{McCormick linearisation.} Introduce auxiliary $w_t = c_g \cdot o_t$ with
three inequalities per hour, using big-M bound $\bar{C} = P$ (demand~MW):

\begin{align}
  w_t &\leq \bar{C}\, o_t               && \label{eq:mc1}\\
  w_t &\leq c_g                          && \label{eq:mc2}\\
  c_g + \bar{C}\, o_t - w_t &\leq \bar{C} && \label{eq:mc3}
\end{align}

At integer $o_t$, these force $w_t = c_g \cdot o_t$ exactly. The gas dispatch
constraints become linear:

\begin{align}
  z_t &\leq w_t           && \forall\,t \label{eq:gas_up}\\
  z_t &\geq \mu\, w_t     && \forall\,t \label{eq:gas_lo}
\end{align}

All capacities ($N_\text{cap} = 4$ at fixed battery duration, or 5 when battery
energy is also free) and all 8,760-h dispatch are jointly optimised in a single
HiGHS call via \texttt{scipy.optimize.milp}.

\textbf{Variable layout} ($N_\text{cap} + 11T$ variables, $T$ binaries):

{\footnotesize
\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{l l >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X}
\toprule
Block & Size & Notes \\
\midrule
Capacities $c_s, c_w, P_b, (E_b), c_g$ & $N_\text{cap}$ & continuous \\
Charge $u_t$, discharge $d_t$, SOC $s_t$ & $3T$ & continuous \\
Gas generation $z_t$ & $T$ & $[0, P]$ \\
Grid buy $g^+_t$, sell $g^-_t$ & $2T$ & box bounded \\
Curtailment $q^s_t$, $q^w_t$ & $2T$ & \\
CFE slack $e_t$ & $T$ & penalised \\
McCormick auxiliary $w_t$ & $T$ & $[0, \bar{C}]$ \\
On/off $o_t$ & $T$ (binary) & integrality\,=\,1 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}}

\smallskip
\textbf{Constraint count:} $13T$ rows — energy balance ($T$, eq.), SOC ($T$, eq.),
CFE ($T$), battery ($3T$), curtailment ($2T$), gas dispatch (\ref{eq:gas_up}--\ref{eq:gas_lo}, $2T$),
McCormick (\ref{eq:mc1}--\ref{eq:mc3}, $3T$).

\textbf{Presolve acceleration.} When both \texttt{max\_solar\_mw} and
\texttt{max\_wind\_mw} are set, $c_s$ and $c_w$ are fixed at their caps
($\ell_b = u_b = \text{cap}$). HiGHS presolve eliminates them before
branch-and-bound begins, tightening the LP relaxation at every B\&B node and
reducing effective problem size.

\textbf{Optimality gap.} The solver halts when
\[
  \frac{\text{incumbent} - \text{LP lower bound}}{\text{incumbent}} \;\leq\; 0.10
\]
(parameter \texttt{mip\_rel\_gap}\,=\,0.10). With fixed VE caps the actual gap at
termination is typically $\ll 1\,\%$ (0.75\,\% in the baseline 2025--2026 run),
well within the 10--20\,\% uncertainty of the underlying cost assumptions. The full
8{,}760-hour MILP solves in approximately 8--9 minutes on a single thread.

% ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection*{Outputs and comparison}

Each scenario returns a \textbf{total annualised cost} (M€/yr): capex$\times$CRF,
fixed O\&M, variable O\&M on dispatched generation, net grid expenditure (purchases
minus export revenue), grid tariffs, and tilslutningsbidrag. LCOE (€/MWh) is total
cost divided by annual demand (8.76\,TWh). The cheapest technology is identified for
each $(x, \text{year})$ pair; the model is run across 2022--2025 and
$x \in \{0.25, 0.50, 0.75\}$.
